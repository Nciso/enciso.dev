## Who I am

I'm Enrique Enciso — a software engineer who builds AI-native products. I care
about systems that are private by default, cheap to run, and honest about what
they know. Most of my work lately sits at the intersection of AI agents,
developer tooling, and identity on the web.

## What I'm building

Right now I'm building **Reley**, a platform that gives businesses an
AI-readable and verifiable identity, and **Jaro**, the studio I build under.
I'm interested in the shift from websites that humans navigate to knowledge
that agents can query directly — this very site is an example of that idea.

## How I think about engineering

I optimize for zero recurring cost and zero surprise. I like static hosting,
build-time work over runtime work, and pushing computation to the edge or the
client whenever it keeps user data private. If a feature can run in the
visitor's browser instead of a server I own, I'll usually choose the browser.

## What I value

- **Grounded answers over confident guesses.** Every claim should trace to a
  source.
- **Private by default.** Data shouldn't leave a device unless the user asks.
- **Boring infrastructure.** The best backend is often no backend.
